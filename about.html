About PharmAvenir

For 30 years we have been dedicated to one mission: making world-class healthcare accessible to every patient, in every corner of the world.

Our Story

From a single laboratory in Mumbai to a global pharmaceutical leader.

PharmAvenir was founded in 1996 by Dr. Vikram Sahni, a visionary scientist who believed that life-saving medicine should never be a luxury. What started with a small team of 12 researchers in a single Mumbai laboratory has grown into a global enterprise employing more than 4,500 people across India, Europe, North America, Africa, and Southeast Asia.

Today, PharmAvenir manufactures over 500 formulations, ranging from essential generics to advanced biosimilars, oncology therapies, and over-the-counter wellness products. Our medicines reach more than 25 million patients each year, and we proudly hold WHO-GMP, US FDA, EU-GMP, TGA, and PIC/S certifications across our 8 manufacturing plants.

But our story is not just about scale — it is about purpose. From running mobile health clinics in rural India to providing free oncology consultations to underserved communities, PharmAvenir continues to put people before profit.

🎯 Our Mission

To deliver high-quality, affordable medicines and healthcare solutions that improve patient outcomes and enhance quality of life — globally and equitably.

👁️ Our Vision

To be the most trusted pharmaceutical partner for patients, healthcare providers, and communities, advancing science to extend healthy lives by 2040.

💎 Our Values

Integrity in every action, science as our compass, compassion at our core, and excellence in everything we deliver.

Our Journey

Three decades of milestones in pharmaceutical innovation.

  • 1996 — PharmAvenir founded in Mumbai with 12 researchers and 5 generic formulations.
  • 2001 — First WHO-GMP certified manufacturing facility opened in Pune.
  • 2005 — Expansion into Africa with offices in Nairobi and Lagos.
  • 2009 — US FDA approval for our flagship cardiovascular line.
  • 2013 — Acquisition of BioGenera Switzerland; entry into biosimilars.
  • 2017 — Launched the PharmAvenir Patient Assistance Program (PAP) — over 2 million beneficiaries to date.
  • 2020 — Produced and donated 50 million doses of essential medicines during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • 2023 — Opened state-of-the-art oncology research center in Boston, USA.
  • 2026 — Celebrating 30 years; launched our carbon-neutral "Green Pharma 2030" initiative.
